\co Reason: Daniel and Stan see the following possibilities for redemptive
themes among the Hunsrik: Grace, Relationship, Forgiveness, Mediator,
Messiah. We're leaning heavily to the Relationship and Mediator themes
initially. The other themes listed above will probably still apear as currents
through the exploratory panorama though they may not be fully developed in
terms of Problem, Prediction, Jesus as the Solution and Response.
\co We'll expand these descriptions in the days and weeks to come.

\co Backtranslator's name or initials

\bld Story 1 -- Creation of the Spirit World
\co We're looking at separating the two creation stories based on length of
telling. Consistently, all of our house churches have asked questions about
this and othe raspects of cosmology.
\cn God - creator of heaven

\bld Story 2 -- Creation of the Physical World
\co This story is the foundaiton for all we know about the relationship that God
desires to have with man. Also relavent to our people is the basis of the
relationship between a husband and wife.
\cn God - creator of earth, Holy Spirit

\bld Story 3 -- The Fall of Mankind
\co This story shows the entrance of a need for a mediator between God and man
and the broken relationship between God and man. This story is actually the
one that introduces the problem related to relationship and mediator. We see
here the seed form of the prediction of the Messiah.

\bld Story 4 -- Covenant with Israel & 10 Commandments
\co This story shows God entering into a relationship with Israel in order to have
a relationship with the other nations. Israel becomes a nation-priest, a
mediating nation, between God and the other nations. The 10 Commandments
will provide a corrective to the Catholic catechism exclusion of having no
"graven images."

\bld Story 5 -- Golden Calf
\co  This story shows Moses as mediator between God and Israel. This story also
addresses the issue of idolatry, praying to the saints, among our people.

\bld Story 6 -- David and Bathsheba w/ Nathan and David
\co This story shows broken relationships on many levels of society and with
God. Nathan's visit may be incuded with this story as well. God sends a
prophet who is something as a  mediator.

\bld Story 7 -- Nathan and David w/ Death of the Child and Psalm 51
\co This story will either start with Nathan's visit or the death of the child,
depending on how the previous story is crafted. We believe the Psalm is vital
to show repentence. This story will address the idea of original sin that we
discovered in Porto Velho about mother's milk passing sin to the child.
\cn Forgiveness of sins

\cn It appears that we have left out the "prediction" story, aside from the story
of the Fall which is very general. We are now aware of this possible gap and
will be considering stories to use for this purpose. We'll let you know when
we decide a story that meets this need in the story set.

\bld Story 8 -- The Birth of Jesus
\co You can't have a redemptive set without this story. There's no way to
understand Jesus without his birth. We may also use this in some way to
counter the original sin issue and mother's milk. We'll see.
\cn Jesus the Son, the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary and forgiveness of
sins

\bld Story 9 -- John the Baptist and Jesus' Baptism
\co John's proclamation was repentence for forgiveness of sin - restoration of
relationship, and making straight the path for the Messiah. Jesus' baptism
deals with one aspect of the baptism barrier we face among our people.

\bld Story 10 -- Parable of the Unjust Servant
\co This story deals with relationship and forgiveness. It is one that has struck a
bell with our people in past studies.

\bld Story 11 -- Parable of the Wedding Feast
\co This story deals with relationship, heaven, and forgiveness.

\bld Story 12 -- Parable of the Prodigal Son
\co This story deals with relationship and forgiveness. It also has struck a bell
with our people in the past. Here we see God as Father.
\cn God the Father

\bld Story 13 -- Crucifixion
\co This story shows Jesus as mediator and the one who pays the price to restore
the relationship that God desires to have with us. This one will probably end
up being 2-3 stories in order to show the arrest/trial and crucifixion.
\cn suffered under Pilate, crucified, died and buried

\bld Story 14 -- Resurrection
\co Without this there is no Christianity, regardless of theme choice.
\cn Raised on the third day and ascended into heaven and seated at the right
hand of the Father

\bld Story 15 -- Peter's Proclamation (Acts 2)
\co This story may be used in conjunction with Acts 1 in two stories. This will
introduce the holy spirit, the church, and the Lord's supper and baptism after
repentence.
\cn Jesus is Lord, church, communion of the saints

\cn After a once-over from the Creed, it seems we need to have a clearer picture
of the bodily resurrection from the dead and the return of Christ to judge the
living and the dead. We'll be looking more closely at these possible gaps in
the exploratory set.

\cn Below is where we will expand the exploratory set for our overall, longer set.
\co Story 1 -- Creation of the Spirit World
\co Story 2 -- Creation of the Physical World
\co Story 3 -- The Fall of Mankind

\anc Gen.03:25
\cn Among the Hunsrik this is significant because their tranditional view of
marital relations is that it is based in sin, i.e. marital, 'sexual', relations are
sinful. In Hunsrik the word for 'sexual relations' literally translates into
English as 'the bads.' This comes from their understanding of their Catholic
heritage which teaches that 'sex' is sinful. A generation or two ago brides
would be given a nuptial lace clothe that was to cover or veil the brides
entire body on her wedding night so as to cover her 'shame.' The only
opening was over the genital area. While this view is changing today among
secularizing forces it still remains an important aspect of culture and needs
to be dealt with. Among the older generation this view is quite strong still and
any public display of affection is viewed with disdain. Naturally in our
Brazilian Latin American context where public display of affection is
demonstrated, if not outright flaunted, their is a conflict between cultural
worldviews. Dealing with this also will set the stage for showing that it is not
sinful for those called to ministry to marry.

\co2 1. place the cursor in the ProjectBT window, click on Database / Insert
Record. 
\co2 2. It will ask you for the Record you want to insert--so you should write a
new chapter number (be sure to insert a zero before single digit
numbers)--try to estimate where the story will come in your story set.
\co2 3. Click ok. 
\co2 4. A new record will open and you can begin to key in the story and anchors. 
\co2 4. The template will automatically give you 30 linenumbers. You can change
them from "02.xx" to whatever chapter number you gave, by using Edit /
Replace. Make sure that you leave the period after the number.
\co2 5. Choose the "replace all" button. Hit "ok".

\co TO SEE YOUR WHOLE LIST OF STORIES
\co2 1. If you want to see titles as well as chapter numbers, click on View /
Browse Fields.  A window will open up with a list of all available fields.
Highlight "t" and click "last" to move it to the right.
\co2 2. Click "ok"
\co2 3. Click on the three-column icon above to see an index of your stories --
both chapter numbers and titles.

\co TO ADD A FIELD:  
\co2 1. Click on Enter, and then "\"-- this brings up a list of Standard Format
markers that this window can use. 
\co2 2. Click on the marker you want, hit space and your cursor will be back in the
window you can write in.

\co TO DELETE A RECORD:  
\co2 1. VERY IMPORTANT:  Click in the window of the record you want to delete.  
\co2 2. Click on Database / Delete record
\co2 3. Select "yes"

\co TO ARRANGE YOUR WINDOWS:  
\co2 1. Click on Window
\co2 2. Choose the type of arrangement you want for your windows (cascade, tile,
tile side by side) -- OR just drag each window to where you want it, and then
resize it to fit.

\co TO SEND A BACKTRANSLATION RECORD TO A CONSULTANT:  
\co2 1. Click in the back-translation window 
\co2 2. Click on File / Export - select "standard format" and select "show
properties dialog box on export" 
\co2 3. Click on the "export" button - another window will open.  If the "consistent
changes" field has anything in it, make it blank.
\co2 4. Hit the "browse" button on the "output file" field - another window will
open 
\co2 5. Give your file a name with a ".txt" extension and hit save. 
\co2 6. Go to your email program and create a new email to your consultant. 
Attach the file that you just exported from Toolbox.

\co TO ADD A RECORD FROM A CONSULTANT:  
\co2 1. Double-click on e-mail attachment to open it.  
\co2 2. Select all the text in the document.  
\co2 3. Copy the highlighted text. 
\co2 4. Click in the Consultant Notes window in Toolbox 
\co2 5. Go to the record number that you are working with - highlight the entire
record. 
\co2 6. Paste to replace the old copy of the file with the new updated one.

\co TO SEND A CONSULTANT NOTES FILE BACK TO THE CONSULTANT
(with your answers in it)
\co2 1. Click in the ConNotes window
\co2 2. Hit "file", then "export" - select "standard format" and select "show
properties dialog box on export" 
\co2 3. Hit the "export" button - another window will open.  Make the "consistent
changes" field blank. 
\co2 4. Hit the "browse" button on the "output file" field - another window will
open 
\co2 6. Go to your email program and create a new email to your consultant. 
Attach the file that you just exported from Toolbox.

\bt backtranslation (keep your lines fairly short)
\bt to JUMP: From this line you can jump to records you have made in the
keyterms database. For example, if this line had the word "God" in it, and if
you have made a keyterm record called "God", then a rightclick on the word
"God" will immediately bring up the record in the keyterms database.
\anc anchor ref -- If you are working with a language for which there is
translated scripture with a backtraslation, it would be helpful to your
consultant for you to paste the BT of the appropriate vss.
\anc to JUMP from anc to NETBIBLE rightclick on the reference here (eg.
Gen.01:01) and your cursor will immediately bring up the verse in
the Netbible window
\cn Crafting note -- put cultural info here, or reasons why you crafted the story
in a certain way, or information about this line from your testing groups.
\cn to JUMP: From this line you can also reference records you have made of
Cultural observations. For example, if this line of the story is about the
Wedding at Cana, and you have made observations about weddings in the
group you work with, A rightclick on the word Wedding will jump to the
"Wedding" record in the Cultural notes database. (Note that in order for the
jump to work, the Cultural Notes database needs to be sorted by \r)
\tst testing question -- prepare testing questions ahead of time.  Then
put the response here also.
\old A preivous version of this line of this line of the story.  As you
proceed through testing and editing of the story you occasionally
decide to delete a line or to make major changes. If you do that,
we'd like for you to keep old versions of each line--especially if
there have been consultant comments about it.
