\r angel
\wrd spirit being, good spirit, powerful spirit
\use Instead of using greek-based term like
"angel", this story set uses a set of more
generic expressions more likely to be
helpful in thinking through key term issues
in a language and cultural setting and
language where key biblical terms may
not yet have been thoroughly processed.  
\ref In 01.03, at the beginning of the panorama a generic expression
"spirit beings" is used.
\dt 19/Nov/2008

\r demon
\use also called "evil spirit"
\ref Introduced in story 01 as spirit beings who
rebelled against God, turned to evil, and
now work in opposition to him.
\dt 19/Nov/2008

\r disobey
\wrd sin, done wrong
\use See Key Biblical Terms document in TW (Translator's Workplace),
where the first sense of "sin" is described as "A specific act or acts
of wrongdoing, disobeying (any of) God's laws"
\ref In story 1, the fallen angels are described as "disobeying" God, in
relation to 2Pe.02.04 saying they "sinned".  (They are also
described as turning to evil, and becoming evil spirits).  Story
02.19 uses the term to characterize what Adam did.
\dt 11/Jan/2007
